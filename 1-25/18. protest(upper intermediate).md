### 1. bankrupt

**Core Meaning**

- Adjective: Unable to pay off debts; completely lacking in a quality (moral, intellectual, etc.)
- Verb: To make someone or a business unable to pay debts; to deplete a resource completely
- Noun: A person or entity legally declared unable to repay outstanding debts

**Key Word Forms**

- `bankruptcy` (n., uncountable/countable): the state of being bankrupt (plural: `bankruptcies`)

**High-Frequency Collocations**

- go bankrupt, declare bankrupt, file for bankruptcy
- personal bankruptcy, corporate bankruptcy, bankruptcy proceedings
- be bankrupt of (ideas/morals/integrity)

**Synonyms & Antonyms**

- Synonyms (adj.): insolvent, broke (informal), destitute
- Antonyms (adj.): solvent, affluent, financially stable

**Example Sentences**

1. The small business went bankrupt after two years of declining sales.
2. The company filed for bankruptcy protection earlier this month.
3. His speech was bankrupt of any real solutions to the crisis.

**Themed Related Vocabulary (Finance & Debt)**

insolvency, liquidation, receivership, debt default, liability, asset, foreclosure, debt ceiling, insolvent, fiscal collapse

### 2. bailout

**Core Meaning**

- Noun (one word): An act of giving financial assistance to a failing business, institution, or economy to prevent its collapse; the act of bailing someone out of jail
- Phrasal Verb (two words: _bail out_):
    
    1. To provide emergency financial support to a failing entity
    2. To help someone out of a difficult situation (informal)
    3. To pay money to release someone from jail
    4. To escape a dangerous situation by parachuting from an aircraft
    

**Key Word Forms**

- `bail` (verb/noun): to secure release from jail; the money paid for release

**High-Frequency Collocations**

- government bailout, financial bailout, bailout package, bailout fund
- bail out a company, bail out of a project, bail sb out of trouble/jail

**Synonyms & Antonyms**

- Synonyms: rescue package, financial rescue, lifeline
- Antonyms: liquidation, default, abandonment

**Example Sentences**

1. The government approved a $70 billion bailout for the struggling airline industry.
2. My parents had to bail me out when I couldn’t pay my rent this month.
3. He decided to bail out of the risky investment before it was too late.

**Themed Related Vocabulary (Economic Policy)**

stimulus package, fiscal aid, federal reserve, systemic risk, too big to fail, quantitative easing, financial crisis, taxpayer money, credit line

### 3. outrage

**Core Meaning**

- Noun: Extreme anger and shock caused by an unfair, cruel, or offensive act; an act that causes such anger
- Verb: To make someone feel extreme anger and shock; to violate a moral or legal principle

**Key Word Forms**

- `outrageous` (adj.): shockingly bad, offensive, or unacceptable
- `outrageously` (adv.)

**High-Frequency Collocations**

- public outrage, spark outrage, cause widespread outrage, moral outrage
- an outrage against justice/human rights, be outraged at/by sth

**Synonyms & Antonyms**

- Synonyms (noun): indignation, fury, uproar, scandal
- Synonyms (verb): enrage, infuriate, offend
- Antonyms (noun): calm, indifference, acceptance
- Antonyms (verb): appease, calm, soothe

**Example Sentences**

1. The decision to cut healthcare benefits sparked public outrage across the country.
2. People were outraged by the company’s blatant disregard for environmental safety.
3. The outrageous price of the new product left many customers refusing to buy it.

**Themed Related Vocabulary (Emotion & Public Reaction)**

indignation, wrath, uproar, outcry, condemnation, scandal, furor, resentment, denunciation, offensive

### 4. have the nerve to do sth

**Core Meaning**

An idiomatic spoken phrase, _primarily used in a negative sense_ to express shock, disapproval, or annoyance at someone’s bold, rude, or disrespectful action. It has a rare neutral usage meaning "to have the courage to do something difficult".

**Alternative Synonymous Phrases**

- have the audacity to do sth (formal)
- have the cheek to do sth (informal, UK)
- have the gall to do sth (informal, US)
- have the courage to do sth (neutral, positive)

**Common Spoken Variants**

- The nerve of you! / What a nerve! (exclamation to express shock at someone’s rudeness)
- How dare you! (stronger alternative)

**Example Sentences**

1. (Negative, most common) He had the nerve to ask for a raise after missing a full month of work.
2. (Negative) She had the nerve to call me lazy when she never helps with anything around the house.
3. (Neutral/Positive) No one else had the nerve to speak up against the unfair rule, so I did.

**Related Spoken Idioms**

- brazen it out, bold as brass, have a lot of front, unashamed, barefaced

### 5. break out

**Core Meaning**

A high-frequency multi-sense phrasal verb, with the most common uses listed below by usage frequency:

1. (Of war, disease, fire, violence, etc.) To start suddenly and unexpectedly
2. To escape from a prison, confined space, or restrictive situation
3. (Of skin) To suddenly become covered in spots, rashes, or acne
4. To suddenly begin an action (laughing, crying, cheering, etc.)
5. To break free from a boring, restrictive routine or cycle

**Key Word Form**

- `breakout` (noun, one word): a sudden start of something (war/disease); an escape from prison; a sudden skin rash; a sudden rise to fame

**High-Frequency Collocations (by sense)**

1. Sudden start: War/fire/pandemic/riots break out
2. Escape: break out of prison/custody, break out of captivity
3. Skin: break out in rashes/acne/bumps
4. Sudden action: break out in tears/laughter/cheers
5. Break free: break out of a routine/cycle/poverty

**Synonyms & Antonyms**

- Synonyms: erupt, start suddenly, escape, break free, burst out
- Antonyms: calm down, suppress, contain, lock up, confine

**Example Sentences**

1. A full-scale war broke out between the two countries just days after the diplomatic talks failed.
2. Three inmates managed to break out of the maximum-security prison overnight.
3. My skin always breaks out when I eat too much dairy.
4. The crowd broke out in cheers when the final score was announced.
5. She quit her office job to break out of the 9-to-5 routine she hated.

**Related Phrasal Verbs with _break_**

break down, break up, break into, break off, break through, break away, break in

### 6. continent

**Core Meaning**

- Noun:
    
    1. One of the world’s seven large continuous land masses
    2. A large continuous area of land, as opposed to islands or the ocean
    3. _the Continent_ (UK English): the mainland of Europe, excluding the UK and Ireland
    
- Adjective (formal): Exercising self-restraint, especially sexually; abstinent

**Key Word Form**

- `continental` (adj.): relating to a continent; typical of mainland Europe

**High-Frequency Collocations**

- the seven continents, the African/Asian/Antarctic continent
- the European continent, continental climate, continental drift, continental shelf
- continental breakfast (light European-style breakfast)

**Standard List of the 7 Continents**

Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, Oceania

**Synonyms & Antonyms**

- Synonyms: landmass, mainland
- Antonyms: island, ocean, islet, archipelago

**Example Sentences**

1. The Amazon rainforest is the largest tropical rainforest on the South American continent.
2. We’re taking a trip around the Continent this summer, visiting France, Italy, and Spain.
3. Continental drift explains how the continents have moved over millions of years.
