## 1. Recession

### Core meaning

A period of temporary economic decline, reduced trade and industrial activity.

### Synonyms

- economic downturn
- slump
- contraction
- slowdown
- depression (more severe)
- crisis

### Collocations

- deep recession
- severe recession
- global recession
- enter a recession
- emerge from a recession
- recession-proof (job/industry)

### Example

Many people lost their jobs during the **recession**.

---

## 2. Loan

### Core meaning

Money borrowed that must be paid back, usually with interest.

### Synonyms

- credit
- advance
- mortgage (special type)
- financing

### Common types

- personal loan
- business loan
- student loan
- car loan
- payday loan
- secured loan / unsecured loan

### Collocations

- take out a loan
- apply for a loan
- repay a loan
- default on a loan
- loan approval
- interest rate on a loan

---

## 3. Mortgage

### Core meaning

A loan to buy property (house/apartment), secured by the property itself.

### Related words

- mortgage rate
- monthly mortgage payment
- mortgage lender
- refinance a mortgage
- down payment
- foreclosure (when bank takes property for non-payment)
- fixed-rate mortgage
- adjustable-rate mortgage (ARM)

### Example

My **mortgage payment** went up because of higher interest rates.

---

## 4. hit me pretty hard

### Meaning

Affected me severely / hurt me a lot / impacted me badly.

### Similar expressions

- took a heavy toll on me
- affected me deeply
- hit hard
- dealt a heavy blow
- took its toll
- hit me where it hurts
- really shook me up

### Example

The job loss **hit me pretty hard**.

---

## 5. on top of that

### Meaning

In addition / furthermore / besides / and to make things worse.

### Synonyms

- moreover
- furthermore
- in addition
- add to that
- to make matters worse
- on top of everything else
- as if that wasn’t enough

### Example

I lost my job, and **on top of that**, my car broke down.
